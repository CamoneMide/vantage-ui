This document contains the granular question bank for the idea-validator skill. Use this during Stage 2 to explore the idea layer by layer.

## Problem Layer
- Does this problem actually exist, or is it a minor inconvenience?
- How frequently does the user encounter this problem?
- What is the cost of inaction (time, money, emotional)?
- Have past attempts been made to solve this? Why did they fail?

## User/Who Layer
- Who is the precise target persona?
- Do they have the spending power to pay for this?
- Who are the early adopters?
- Are they easily reachable through existing channels or communities?

## Solution & Alternatives Layer
- What are the current workarounds? (Excel, manual labor, ignoring it)
- Who is the closest direct or indirect competitor?
- What is the specific "job-to-be-done" from the user's perspective?

## Uniqueness & Moat Layer
- Is this defensible? How easy is it for a competitor to clone?
- Is this a feature, a product, or a company? 
- What proprietary data, network effect, or brand advantage can be built over time?

## Constraints & Risks Layer
- **Technical**: Are there hard limits on performance, API constraints, or scaling bottlenecks?
- **Regulatory**: Are there legal or compliance hurdles (HIPAA, GDPR, FINRA)?
- **Timing**: Is the market ready for this right now? Why now?
- **Behavioral**: Does this require the user to change deeply ingrained habits?
- **Economic**: Does the customer acquisition cost (CAC) make sense given the lifetime value (LTV)?
- **Chicken-and-egg**: For marketplaces or platforms, how do you solve the cold start problem?

## Viability & Evidence Layer
- What is the smallest test that can be run to prove demand?
- What is the single biggest leap-of-faith assumption?
- What specific metric would prove this assumption true?
